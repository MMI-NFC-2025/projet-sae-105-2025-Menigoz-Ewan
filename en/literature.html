Women in Science in Literature

Remarkable Creatures

Tracy Chevalier (2009)

Historical novel exploring class and gender barriers through Mary's relationship with Elizabeth Philpot.

The Fossil Hunter

Shelley Emling (2009)

Factual biography detailing her expertise, discoveries, and difficult life.

Stone Girl, Bone Girl

Laurence Anholt (1999)

Children's book about Mary Anning.

The Dragon in the Cliff

Sheila Cole (1991)

Youth fiction based on her life.

Themes Addressed

Recognition of female scientists

These books restore the visibility of women whose names have been erased from the history of science.

The Matilda Effect

They shed light on the systemic denial of women's contributions in favor of male colleagues.

Inspiration for new generations

By highlighting the journeys of these women, these works encourage girls to pursue scientific careers.
